Origami Instructions


Origami is the art of paper folding, which is often associated with Japanese culture.
In modern usage, the word "origami" is used as an inclusive term for all folding practices,
regardless of their culture of origin.

Camel

Intersting Facts About Camel

  1. There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  2. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  3. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

Chameleon

Intersting Facts About Chameleon

  1. Chameleons are reptiles that are a part of iguana suborder.
  2. Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameleons.
  3. Most Chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches.

Pigeon

Intersting Facts About Pigeons

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  2. Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities. They use a range of skills, such as using the sun as a guide and an internal ‘magnetic compass’.
  3. Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.

Panda

Intersting Facts About Pandas

  1. Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown)
  2. Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  3. Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.

Teddy-Bear

Intersting Facts About Teddy bears

  1. The teddy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a 1902 hunting trip.
  2. A teddy has been into space! Magellan T Bear boarded Space Shuttle Discovery in 1995 as part of a school project.
  3. in Germany in the same year, the Steiff family developed a silk-like fabric bear, based on zoo animals. They caused a sensation at a toy fair and were snapped up by an American buyer.

Cicada

Intersting Facts About Cicadas

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies
  2. The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
  3. Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes.
